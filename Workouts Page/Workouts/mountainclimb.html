Mountain Climber

Mountain Climbers are a full-body exercise that targets the core, arms, and legs. This high-intensity move helps to improve cardiovascular endurance, agility, and strength, while burning fat and toning muscles.

  • Core (Abs, Obliques)
  • Arms (Shoulders, Triceps)
  • Legs (Quads, Hamstrings, Calves)

How to Perform Mountain Climbers:

  1. Start in a plank position with your arms directly under your shoulders and your body in a straight line.
  2. Bring one knee toward your chest, then quickly switch legs, alternating in a running motion.
  3. Keep your core tight and your back flat as you continue alternating legs quickly for 30-60 seconds.

Focus on engaging your core to maintain a stable posture throughout the movement. Increase speed as you become more comfortable.
