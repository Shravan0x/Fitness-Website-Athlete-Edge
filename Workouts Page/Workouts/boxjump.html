Box Jumps

Box Jumps are a plyometric exercise that involves jumping onto a sturdy box or platform, helping to improve lower body power, explosiveness, and coordination. This exercise is commonly used in training for athletes to develop strength, agility, and speed.

  • Quadriceps
  • Glutes
  • Hamstrings
  • Calves
  • Core (for stabilization)

Box jumps are a great way to increase lower body strength and explosive power, while also improving your cardiovascular endurance. They are often used in HIIT, CrossFit, and functional fitness routines.

How to Perform Box Jumps:

  1. Start by standing with your feet shoulder-width apart, about a foot or two away from a sturdy box or platform.
  2. Engage your core and bend your knees slightly to prepare for the jump, making sure your hips, knees, and ankles are aligned.
  3. Explosively jump upward, swinging your arms forward for momentum, and land with both feet on the box or platform.
  4. When landing, absorb the impact by slightly bending your knees and landing softly with your entire foot on the box. Keep your chest upright and your core engaged.
  5. Step back down carefully, one foot at a time, and reset before performing the next jump.
  6. Repeat for the desired number of repetitions.

Focus on landing softly to reduce the impact on your joints. Start with a low box and gradually increase the height as you become more comfortable and confident with the movement.
